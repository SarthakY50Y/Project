ESA

The European Space Agency (ESA) is a joint space organisation formed by multiple European countries. It was established in 1975 and is headquartered in Paris, France.

ESAâ€™s main objective is to ensure that Europe remains competitive in space science and technology. It works in areas such as planetary exploration, Earth observation, satellite navigation, and space transportation.

ESA has contributed important missions like Rosetta, which landed a probe on a comet, Gaia, which maps billions of stars, and Copernicus, a major Earth-observation programme. ESA is also a key partner in the International Space Station.

ESA develops launch vehicles such as Ariane rockets and collaborates with other agencies including NASA, ISRO, and JAXA.

By combining the resources of many nations, ESA demonstrates how international cooperation can lead to major scientific achievements.
